History of Khmer New Year

Khmer New Year, also known as "Choul Chnam Thmey," is celebrated annually in April. It marks the end of the harvest season and the beginning of the new year according to the Khmer calendar. The celebration lasts for three days, each with its own rituals and significance.

During this time, Cambodians visit temples, offer prayers, and participate in traditional games. It's a time for family reunions, feasts, and cultural festivities.
